About Sign Language Recognition
Overview

This tool provides real-time recognition of manual alphabet gestures (A-F) using computer vision techniques. It demonstrates the fundamental principles of sign language detection and serves as an educational resource for understanding how technology can bridge communication gaps.

Five Parameters of Sign

Sign languages are complex, natural languages with their own grammar and syntax. The five parameters are essential components that distinguish one sign from another:

  • Hand shape: The specific configuration of fingers and hands
  • Location: Where the sign is produced relative to the body
  • Movement: The action and motion of the hands
  • Palm Orientation: Direction the palm faces
  • Non-Manual Features: Facial expressions and body posture
Important Facts
  • Grammatical Structure: Sign languages have unique grammar distinct from spoken languages
  • Iconicity: Many signs bear resemblance to objects or actions they represent
  • Non-Universal: Each deaf community has its own sign language (ASL ≠ SASL)
Current Capabilities

This demonstration recognizes hand shapes for letters A through F using contour analysis and convexity defects. Additional parameters can be enabled in Settings for enhanced analysis.
